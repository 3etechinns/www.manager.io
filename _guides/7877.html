A title is the name of a particular transaction form in Manager. Titles appear near the top of forms in many themes, but can be placed elsewhere. They include:

  • Payments, receipts, and transfers
  • Expense claims
  • Sales and purchase invoices
  • Sales quotes
  • Sales and purchase orders
  • Credit and debit notes
  • Delivery notes and goods receipts
  • Payslips
  • Production orders
  • Journal entries
  • Customer and supplier statements

The title of a single form can be changed by modifying a custom theme. If you have not created a custom theme yet, follow instructions in another Guide, then these steps:

  • Under the Settings tab, click Themes. Then click Edit next to the theme you want to modify:

=> =>

  • When the custom theme's computer code appears, locate the following segment of code: {{title}}. (You may have to scroll to the right to see complete lines.)

  • Replace that code with {{title | replace: "Old Title", "New Title"}}. Preserve all other code in the same line. "Old Title" must be the name of the form you want to change, exactly as it appears when using the default theme. "New Title" is the text you wish to substitute. Both are case sensitive, and the quotation marks must be included.

  • Click Update.

Any time forms are viewed in the particular custom theme, old title text will be replaced by new title text.

Example 1
The title of a sales invoice can be converted from Invoice to Tax Invoice by substituting this code into the appropriate line: 

{{title | replace: "Invoice", "Tax Invoice"}}

CAUTION
Beware of unintended consequences. Any place old title text appears in the title of any form, it will be replaced with new title text. In the first example above, not only would Invoice become Tax Invoice, but Purchase Invoice would become Purchase Tax Invoice. So you may wish to limit usage of custom themes to specific forms or create additional themes. You might also use more sophisticated conditional tests in your code. Those are beyond the scope of this Guide.

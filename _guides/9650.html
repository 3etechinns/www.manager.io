To record movement of money from one cash account of a business entity to another, use the Transfer money operation in the Cash Accounts tab. Examples of transfers include:

  • Withdrawing cash from a bank account for a petty cash fund
  • Paying off a business credit card from a company bank account
  • Moving money from a general purpose bank account to a payroll account

Note
Transfers can be instantaneous, as when moving money online between accounts in the same bank. Or they may remain pending for several days, as when mailing a cheque to pay a credit card statement.

In the Cash Accounts tab, click Transfer money:



Complete the entry (some fields appear as you progress):

  • Date will be prefilled with today's date. This can be edited.
  • The optional Reference field can be used for anything. Typically, a transaction or confirmation number is entered.
  • Description allows you to record purpose of the transfer.
  • Paid from records the origin account.
  • Amount records the value of the transaction in your base currency.
  • Status can be Pending or Cleared, in which case a date can be entered.
  • Received in records the destination account.
  • Amount received will automatically match the amount transferred. If this is changed, the Amount for the origin account automatically changes to match.
  • Status options are the same for the destination account as the origin account.

Click Create to finalize the transfer:

Caution
Remember, the Transfer money operation only records a transfer. It does not actually move any money. You must take action to withdraw and deposit, make the online transfer, or send the cheque.

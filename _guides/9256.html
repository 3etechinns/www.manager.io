Custom control accounts can be useful additions to your chart of accounts. A control account in Manager is a top-level, balance sheet account containing subsidiary ledgers or subaccounts. (The terms are interchangeable). Some accounts activated automatically when various tabs are enabled are control accounts. Perhaps the most familiar example is Cash & cash equivalents, the default control account for cash accounts. Another good example is Accounts receivable, made up of individual customer ledgers.

A custom control account is one you create yourself to segregate and give visibility to selected subsidiary ledgers that would otherwise be assigned to a built-in control account. A custom control account may contain only one type of subsidiary ledger, but all the same possibilities as for built-in control accounts are allowed:

  • Cash accounts
  • Customers
  • Suppliers
  • Inventory items
  • Fixed assets
  • Intangible assets

Examples
Jurgen operates a machine shop with some very expensive, digitally controlled boring and milling machines. He wants see the value of these important assets on his balance sheet separately from other fixed assets like workbenches and office furniture. So he creates a custom control account called Digital machinery and assigns those fixed assets to it.

Northwind Traders has a variety of cash accounts it uses for different purposes. Rather than leave them all lumped together under the built-in control account, Cash & cash equivalents, it decides to break them apart for better management visibility. So the company renames that account as Cash deposits. It creates two new custom control accounts, Payroll accounts and Term deposits. Northwind leaves its various demand deposit and petty cash funds in the original, renamed account. It assigns the accounts at local branch banks used to pay employees in various cities to Payroll accounts. And it assigns its longer term certificates of deposit to Term deposits. Now the balance sheet clearly distinguishes between highly liquid funds, money available for the weekly payroll, and cash reserves that are not readily accessible.

Create a custom control account

To create custom control account, go toSettings Chart of Accounts and click New Account:

Define the custom control account:

  • Name the account as you want it to appear on your balance sheet.
  • Enter a numerical Code to control display sequence.
  • Select which Group the custom control account will be in: Assets, Liabilities, or Equity.
  • Choose a Tax Code if one applies to most transactions to be posted to the account. (This will be editable when recording transactions. The field will not be visible if you have no tax codes defined.)
  • Check the box for Control account.
  • Select the type of subsidiary ledgers the control account will be Made up of.

Click Create to add the custom control account to your chart of accounts.

Note
When you check the box to indicate a control account, the box for indicating a starting balance disappears (if you have a start date set). This is because control accounts do not have starting balances. Any carryovers from a previous accounting system are recorded at the subaccount level.

Example
Brilliant Industries has defined a number of fixed assets. It has not defined any tax codes. Nor has it set a start date. By default, the purchase costs of all fixed assets are shown on the balance sheet under the Fixed assets account (a built-in control account). The company wants to split fixed assets into categories and have each category show separately as:

- Land & Buildings
- Motor Vehicles
- Furniture

Once you have created a custom control account made up of a specific subaccount type, you will be able to select control accounts for all subaccounts of the same type when creating new ones or by editing existing ones.

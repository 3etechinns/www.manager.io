Your customer has requested an invoice from you. What is it? It is basically a list of goods sent or services provided, with a statement of the sum due for these. Basically a bill. For accounting purposes, many customers won't pay until invoice is presented to them.

So let's say you are running a computer repair business. Before you can starting issuing invoices to customers, you need to set up yourself as a business in Manager.

Create a business

Click Add Business button then Create New Business, enter your business name and click Add Business

Your business should be created but it will look empty.

Customizing left navigation

Since every business is different, all modules will be disabled by default. To issue invoice to customer, we will need to enable Customers and Sales Invoices tabs.

Click Customize and enable these two tabs so they are added to your left navigation.

Make sure to click Update button at the bottom to save your choice.

Now we are getting somewhere, your left navigation will contain two new tabs: Customers and Sales Invoices.

Adding a customer

Before you can issue your first invoice, you need to add at least one customer.

Click on Customers tab.

Then click New Customer button.

Enter customer details.

And then click Create button to save the customer.

Your customer is now created as you can see in the list of customers.

Now you are ready to issue an invoice to this customer.

Issuing an invoice to customer

Click Sales Invoices tab.

Then click New Sales Invoice button.

This will open new sales invoice form where you need to fill in at least three fields:

  • Customer
  • Description
  • Unit Price

Click Create button to save the invoice.

You will be instantly taken to preview of your invoice.

A few notes:

  • Unit price doesn't show because you didn't enter any Qty. Manager will always try to make the invoice as simple as possible. If you are not using Qty column, Manager won't show Qty and Unit Price columns on invoice. It will show Amount column only.
  • By default, your business address will be fictional. You should click on Settings, then Business Details to provide your real business address so it will be shown on the invoice.
  • If you are charging tax such as GST, VAT, sales tax or similar, you need to go to Settings tab, then Tax Codes to create relevant tax codes. Then Edit this invoice to select the right tax code.
  • If you want to show on invoice payment information, you can put them into Notes field when editing the invoice.
  • If you would like to show your business logo, go to Settings, then Business Logo

When viewing the invoice, also notice Switch Theme button in top-right corner.

This will allow you change the appearance of your invoice.
